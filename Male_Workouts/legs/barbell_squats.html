Barbell Squats

The Barbell Squat or also known as the Barbell Back Squat is a compound exercise that activates muscle groups throughout the lower body including hamstring, glutes and lower back muscles.

3 sets of 10 to 12 repetitions
Target Muscle:
Instructions:
1. Stand with your feet more than shoulder-width apart, this wide stance will allow a deeper squat, getting your glutes and hamstrings involved.
2. Hold the barbell across your upper back with an overhand grip, avoid resting it on your neck. Hug the bar into your traps to engage your upper back muscles.
3. Drive your heels into the floor to push yourself explosively back up. Keep form until you’re stood up straight: that’s one and then repeat.
Benefits: Front squats give you all the benefits of a regular back squat but take some of the pressure away from your back and knees, meaning that you can train for longer.
