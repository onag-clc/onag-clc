Incline Dumbbell Curls

Dumbbell curls are some of the most basic strength-training exercises you can learn. If you’re ready to mix up your routine and work different arm muscles, you might consider adding incline dumbbell curls to your workout.

3 sets of 4 to 8 repetitions
Target Muscle:
Instructions:
1. Sit down against the workout bench, keeping your back straight and your abdominal muscles tight. Your weights should be at your sides to start, one in each hand.
2. When you’ve gotten the starting position down, lift each dumbbell, palms up, toward your shoulders.
3. Slowly lower the dumbbells back down to your starting position. Don’t release the weights too fast or you could strain your muscles. This should be a controlled movement. Repeat.
Benefits: The incline dumbbell curl elongates the negative portion of the dumbbell curl, creating a deeper stretch on the biceps muscle tissue.
