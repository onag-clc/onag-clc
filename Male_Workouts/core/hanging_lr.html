Hanging Leg Raise

Hanging leg raises require the use of a bar that you can grasp above your head. This bar must be stable and able to support your full body weight, as well as withstand the stress that is added when you raise and lower your legs.

3 sets of 8 to 12 repetitions
Target Muscle:
Instructions:
1. Grab the bar with an overhand grip, your thumbs wrapped around the bar to improve stability. Lift your feet off the ground, raising your straight legs outward in front of you.
2. Raise your legs to a level that feels challenging but still enables you to keep good form. Strive to get them parallel to the ground.
3. Lower your legs back down slowly until they return to the starting position. Maintain your posterior pelvic tilt, even at the bottom of the movement. Repeat.
Benefits: Both primary hip flexors—the sartorius and the iliopsoas—work hard during hanging leg raises. The sartorius assists with hip and knee flexion.
